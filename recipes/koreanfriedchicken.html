Korean Fried Chicken

Korean fried chicken is a type of fried chicken that is typically coated in a thick, sweet and savory batter. It is often served with a variety of dipping sauces, such as soy sauce, honey mustard, or chili garlic sauce. Korean fried chicken is typically made with boneless, skinless chicken breast or thigh fillets. The chicken is first marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sugar, garlic, ginger, and other spices. It is then coated in a thick batter made from flour, cornstarch, eggs, and water. The chicken is then deep-fried until golden brown and crispy.

Korean fried chicken is often served with a variety of side dishes, such as french fries, kimchi, or pickled vegetables. It is also a popular bar food in Korea, and is often served with beer or soju.

Ingredients

  1. Marinade

    • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken thighs, quartered
    • ½ yellow onion, grated
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon fine salt
    • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

  2. Other

    • 4 cups oil for frying, or as needed

  3. Batter

    • ¾ cup cornstarch
    • ½ cup self-rising flour
    • 1 teaspoon white sugar
    • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • 1 cup very cold water, or as needed

Steps

  1. Make marinade: Stir together chicken, onion, garlic, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl until chicken is coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate, 4 hours to overnight.
  2. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 340 degrees F (171 degrees C).
  3. While oil is heating, make batter: Whisk together cornstarch, flour, sugar, pepper, and salt in a large bowl. Gradually whisk in cold water until mixture resembles smooth pancake batter. Use tongs to remove chicken from marinade to batter; stir to coat chicken completely. Discard marinade.
  4. Working in batches, fry chicken in hot oil for 4 minutes. Transfer chicken to a cooling rack.
  5. Increase oil temperature to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  6. Working in batches, fry chicken again in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to drain.
